Resignation Letter Format and Samples

Saying goodbye to your teammates and manager is never an easy task. But a courteous resignation letter can mean the divergence between a discomfited goodbye, and the possibility for long professional relation.

A resignation letter is an authorized document sent by an employee to his/her employer or manager giving notice they will no longer be functioning at the company. The main objective of resignation is to make an official record of notice, summarize any next steps, give details about the employee’s last day and continue an optimistic relationship with the employer.

So, below we have assembled some of the important information regarding the resignation letter format, which is essential for an employee to acquire when writing a resignation letter to an employer/manager.

What Does The Resignation Letter Format Include?

Some essential factors which are part of the resignation letter format:

  • Your Name & Address
  • Letter date
  • Company Address
  • Addressee
  • A declaration that clearly states you’re going to resign
  • Mention date of your last working day (based on the notice you’re giving)
  • A short brief about why you’re leaving the job
  • Appreciation to your employer for hiring you
  • In the end, polite thank you note
  • Signature
  • Your contact information

Some Useful Tips For Writing A Resignation Letter

Adopting an appropriate resignation letter format can be utilized best with the following tips:

  • Inform and discuss the resignation with your reporting manager and/or HR executive beforehand.
  • Address the required person/people and state a simple, clear, and neutral reason for the resignation.
  • Mention the notice period you are serving and invite discussion over it.
  • Mention your gratitude and thanks for the support and learning. Do not go specific, express generally to the entire organization and your department/team.
  • Close the letter on a positive note like "Hope to work with you again." "Best wishes and regards for your futures." and so on.
  • Provide signature and date if submitted in print/writing. Also, keep a counter-signed copy with yourself.

Why Should You Write a Resignation Letter?

Since the resignation letter is addressed to and accessed by some key senior personnel, it is important to keep it short, simple, and objective. The professional approach ensures no misinterpretation or communication mistakes occur. This consolidates a good image and relationships with the organization and members and also, helps positively in transition, referral, background check, and other requirements of the job change. Often, it is required to be submitted to the next organization hiring the individual. The formats of resignation letter are designed to cater to these need of the document.

Types of Resination Letters

There are no standard types of resignation letter format or content but they can be bifurcated into several types based on the use case. Print and non-print resignation letters are one of them and don't need any explanation. Also, resignation letters can be divided based on whether or not the process is permanent or temporary; it is specific to the situation. Temporary resignation is often processed when an employee leaves due to non-professional reasons like medical care, education, personal problems, etc and is uncertain of joining back but wishes to. In this case only, one should specify the reason and seek permission to join if they can later. Here are the other important types:

Resignation Letter Format with Notice Period

Any termination of employment is usually done with a transition period called notice period. This period serves many functions and is considered a norm while resignation. The notice period depends on employers policy and is usually a minimum of two weeks.

  • Always specify the period in your resignation letter clearly.
  • It is best to discuss the notice period with HRD before sending the letter.
  • Provide all the necessary details that can help with a smooth transition and documentation.
Use Case:

Resignation while changing jobs, for academic purposes, or any professional transition without negative effects on career.

Resignation Letter Format without Notice Period

Also called Immediate Resignation Letter, this kind of resignation is used to terminate employment within a very short period. This period is usually less than a week. It is more declarative and should be used with care, possibly not used at all.

Download Print Version Here Points to Consider:
  • Specify the exact date of leaving the job. You may hold back on the reason to leave though.
  • Discuss the resignation before sending in the letter.
  • Request a properly documented and permitted resignation to nullify the negative effects.
  • An immediate resignation may harshly affect the career and financial side so it is best avoided.
Use Case:

Medical Emergency, Personal Problems or Dependencies, or any kind of emergency may demand it and possibly not affect the career in the long run. However, professional transitions and other such changes should not be handled with such resignation but can be.

Warning:

Immediate resignation is often considered as absconding a job. It may also lead to loss of pay, experience certificate, and references from the employer.
