Resume Format

A Resume is a summarised document which represents a job seeker’s professional background and skills for a prospective employer. A resume format usually describes an individual’s work experience, qualifications, and relevant skills about a job application. A perfect resume format will successfully pass applicant tracking systems which aims to access resume and other details electronically.

  • Freshers

  • Experienced

Header

A header enables the recruiters to know your basic contact details and where you are at present. A header should be clearly and neatly placed right on top of the resume – ideally centre aligned. You header should include:

Name: Make sure your name is in bold and a bigger font than the rest of the resume.
E-mail ID: Do not share your work e-mail, but a personal one. However, make sure it sounds professional and unlike sweetguy@gmail.com or so.
Phone and mobile number: It's best to list just one phone number. Make sure you add the code before a landline number. Only give a number on which you would be available during office hours.

OBJECTIVE

Convey your goal to your employer and aim towards a particular position in a specific industry in about 15 words. It should maximum go into 2 lines for middle to senior level candidates. It's important to customize the resume objective to match the position you are applying for. Customize your objective according to the job role you apply to. Keep in mind the employer’s requirement and be specific about your aim.

PROFILE SUMMARY

A profile summary is the synopsis of your career. This section is your chance to convince the prospective employer that you have the skills necessary for the job, held responsible positions marked with achievements in the past, and that your career has progressed over the years.

For freshers, profile summary can be of 5 to 7 pointers. Explain your + points and skills in a clear and concise manner. Do not fake any achievements or projects. Begin by your educational achievements and proficiencies. Add your expertise and skills in an effective manner.

ACADEMIC PROJECTS

This section is considered to be an important section which determines your projects in detail. Since you are not experienced, it is essential for you to show the kind of work you can produce for upcoming organizations. Your examples will show you the capabilities in different areas.

Mention the projects individually and describe in short about your responsibilities over the period of time. Put all relevant information about the projects and present it in a clear and concise manner.

EDUCATION

Your educational qualifications have a lot of effect on the recruiters’ decision to consider you for the job. The best approach in this case will be to list your qualifications in a functional manner, so that the recruiters see the most relevant ones on the top. This way, recruiters get the required information in just a glance. Mention your educational details starting from the most recent one.

Enlist life experiences like a short stint as a voluntary social worker, community service efforts as they are beneficial even if not related to the job. These can actually be translated to job skills. Listing coursework and awards also work to your advantage if they are relevant to the job you are applying for. Pay attention to areas where it fits the job's requirements. If you have any extra-curricular activities to boost your skills, mention them.

SKILLS

Understand the requirement of the job vacancies you are interested in applying for. This exercise will help you outline the skill demands of those job openings and make it easier for you to list the most important skills you must highlight as you build your resume.

Use this section to:

  • Outline your expertise
  • Target your resume to a particular job opening by including keywords that define the particular job vacancy.
  • Highlight soft skills
  • Showcase transferable skills

EXTRA ACTIVITIES

For a fresher, it is essential to showcase extra curriculum activities. Listing your accomplishments in the resume not only showcases your candidature in a stronger light, it will also reinforce confidence in you about your career track.

PERSONAL DETAILS

There are no set rules for this section. Give the details you are comfortable with. For example, if you are worried about your age then you can skip this detail in the resume. Include details like:

  • Date of birth
  • Language known
  • Marital Status
  • Languages Known

In case of references, if you do not have any while submitting the resume then it is perfectly fine. Be ready, however, to face these questions at the time of interview.

Types of Resume formats and their use

From traditional format to a highly creative one, there are many proper resume formats from which you can choose. However, there are three basic formats which are widely accepted amongst recruiters. They are chronological, functional, and combination resume format. Another class of formats which are in consideration are creative and visual resume format.

CHRONOLOGICAL RESUME FORMATS

The chronological resume format is one of the most traditional and recognisable formats. Recruiters prefer this format because of its familiar and simple layout. The information in the resume is presented in what is referred to as ‘reverse-chronological’ order which lists your recent to old experience through the time. Chronological resume format is the most popular structure which is used by many job seekers. They prefer this format because it encompasses all industries and experience levels. The only reason where you should not use this format is if you have multiple career gaps and frequency changed jobs. Choosing a chronological resume format for right reasons will surely impact your chances of standing out from other candidates.

Features of a Chronological Resume Format:

  • Simple, easy to go through, and detailed in nature.
  • Used to highlight work history from recent to oldest.
  • Enhances the way to showcase experience and achievements.
  • Best to convert into a detailed CV.

Recommended for:

Experienced professionals (2+ years of experience), and graduates or above applying for higher education.

FUNCTIONAL RESUME FORMATS

A functional resume format is known for a skill based resume which emphasises on qualifications, skills, and accomplishments. The work history is generally listed at the bottom of the resume and is less elaborated in comparison to the chronological resume format. Moreover, this format does not follow a defined pattern, and recruiters generally find it the way to hide shortcomings. Although, that is not the primary motive of this resume format. Besides, it allows candidates from all backgrounds to portray their best foot forward. If you are using this resume format, remember to emphasize on your abilities and experience in a clear manner which will surely give you a best shot at interviews.

Features of a Functional Resume Format:

  • Simple format with direct and factual information.
  • Used to highlight skills, academic achievements, co-curricular achievements, etc.
  • Versatile and can be made with tables, bullet points, or any other similar formatting.
  • Easy to go through and doesn’t contain historical details.

Recommended for:

Freshers, undergraduate students applying for internships, college, etc. Back/front office job applications, part-time job applications.

COMBINATION RESUME FORMATS

The combination resume format, also known as the hybrid resume format has gained popularity with its customizable yet practical form. You can showcase your skills at the top while using a chronological format which gives detail to your career path.

Features of a combination resumes Format

  • Used to highlight experience, learning, and achievements with an analytical/storytelling/detailed approach.
  • Versatile with the customisation that can be made but follows a list-based content approach.
  • Difficult to create and read as well. However, this resume format should be customised according to a job application.

Recommended for:

Experienced professionals, freelancers and candidates looking for a higher position.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. What is a resume format?

A resume format is a sample resume that can be edited and filled with the required details. It is often provided with instructions or sample text and needs a rigorous edit to make it useful.

2. I’ve never created a resume before, how do I make a resume for the first time?

Use a word template to start your resume and choose amongst the type of resumes mentioned above. Make your resume legible, readable and well managed with relevant titles and headings.

3. How to choose a resume format?

There are various kinds of resume formats that can be used. You can also develop your customized format from scratch. The format should provide the required information strategically and clearly. Also, based on various factors like experience, skills, industry, etc, you can choose between a chronological resume format, a functional resume format, or a combination resume format.
  • A chronological resume is used when you have been working in the same industry and you are applying for another job within the same space.
  • A functional resume is used if you are a new graduate and have very less or no work experience.
  • A combination resume is used when if you have skills developed outside your area of expertise, have career gaps or changing profession.

4. Why are resume formats important?

Resume formats are important, especially the resume types that are mainstream due to their simplicity, targeting, and utility with job applications and their processing. A good resume is easily legible, readable and well managed with relevant titles and headings. Different resume formats are also important to show information in different ways to emphasize the connection of a candidate with the job profile, organization, and industry.

5. What makes a resume good and attractive?

A resume is considered good if it looks neat, simple, well-managed, and is easy to read and skim through without missing important information. Simple text fonts in a maximum of two colors and three sizes are the basic requirements. Distributing information in shorter points, giving proper headings, aligning text on the left margin and other best practices make the resume look good and attractive. Composing simpler sentences, using appropriate vocabulary, highlighting or underlining important phrases, and similar practices with content will make it readable and legible. You can also use some creativity with these but keep it limited and uncompromising on its utility.

6. Should I include hobbies in my resume?

One should only list hobbies if they are professionally relevant. Make sure the hobbies project skills and personality traits related to the job you are applying for. You can take advantage of this section if you have achievements to quote with your hobbies.

7. What makes a resume bad or unattractive? What not to do in a resume?

There are several factors that can make a resume bad or unattractive; the worst is unmanaged and badly formatted text. Standard resume formats solve this problem beforehand. Apart from it, recursive or multiple grammatical and spelling mistakes can result in bad impressions. Using odd fonts, uneven text alignment, long paragraphs (<4-5 sentences), long sentences (<22 words), etc are a strict no-no. Do not include your picture and other unnecessary information. Do not make your resume longer than two A4 size pages. But also, do not cramp the text in the document; leave ample spaces between lines, paragraphs, and other elements in the resume.

8. Should I include an objective statement in my resume?

Every employer has a different perspective. We suggest if you are including objective in your resume, make sure it is directed and specific to the employer. Also, objective statements work better without the heading 'Objective' or something else.

9. How many pages are allowed in a resume?

Employers prefer resumes with 1 or 2 pages. Moreover, for freshers, a single-page resume is acceptable and for experienced two pages resume serves the purpose. In case of highly experienced, one more page can be added but it is best to not extend it further.

Resume formatting and layout tips

Here are few tips you should consider when you want to create your resume format to stand out from other candidates:

  • Present your work history in an order
  • Try and avoid long paragraphs and lengthy bullet lists
  • Avoid too much white space.
  • Courier, Times New Roman, Helvetica, and Arial are some of the readable fonts.
  • Never use double spaces between words and use just a single line space between lines.
  • Use 0.5 inches to 1 inch of margins to give your resume a neat look.
  • Avoid using a table with multiple columns.
  • Don’t include graphics, background images or pictures in your resume
  • Ensure that there are no spelling or grammatical errors in your resume.
  • Ensure that you have just the relevant information in your resume and not a complete laundry list.

Difference Between Resume, Curriculum Vitae (CV) and Biodata

Resumes, CV and Biodata are very similar in nature and are used to present personal-professional information about a person. They are usually used in job applications, academic applications, and similar documentation. The primary differences between the three are:

  • Biodata is a very short document with basic factual information only while resume and CV present the same information in more details. Curriculum Vitaes contain very detailed information while resumes have brief descriptions only.
  • Biodata formats are single page while resume formats can extend to two pages and CV formats can be even longer due to the details presented. However, it is not recommended to have a CV longer than 3-pages unless you are highly experienced.
  • Biodata is used widely and may be required for documentation other than jobs or academic. Resumes and CVs are mainly used for job applications and academic applications or similar professional use.
  • Biodata follows the format of a functional resume while CVs can be made with either combination resume or chronological resume.
