Resume Format and CV Samples

The resume is one of those documents that represent your professional information, mostly to depict one's professional growth and expertise. It is usually submitted to and accessed by professionals like hiring managers, recruiters, organizational heads, etc. To standardize either of the processes, resume formats are introduced for different needs and specifications like academic, job search (industry specific), freshers resume format and so on. It makes the presentation of information streamlined and simple, giving an edge to both sides in hiring and other forms of decision making.

Types of Resume formats and their use

There are three basic types of resume formats that are considered standard as per industry norms. These resume formats are devised to simplify the application process by standardizing the important information and presenting in an easy-to-access manner. Apart from these, there is another class of resume formats called creative resume format or visual resume format. Every format has unique features that make them useful in different cases. Here's how:

CHRONOLOGICAL RESUME FORMATS

The chronological resume is the type of format in which information is presented in a standard timeline, preferably reverse chronological. It means that the information is presented from recent to old or vice versa, as the events happened.

Features of a Chronological Resume Format:

  • The chronological resume is a simple resume, easy to go through, and detailed in nature.
  • This type of resume is used to highlight work history from recent to oldest.
  • It enhances the way experience and achievements are presented in the resume.
  • It is the best resume format to convert into a detailed CV.

Recommended for:

Experienced professionals (2+ years of experience), professionals applying for a similar profile as current, and graduates or above applying for higher education.

FUNCTIONAL RESUME FORMATS

The functional resume is used to present direct points with an emphasis on factual, static, and simple information. The information does not follow a defined pattern except as defined by headings.

Features of a Functional Resume Format:

  • The functional resume is a simple format with direct and factual information.
  • This type of resume is used to highlight skills, academic achievements, co-curricular achievements, etc.
  • It is very versatile and can be made with tables, bullet points, or any other similar formatting.
  • It is easiest to go through and doesn’t contain narrative details.

Recommended for:

Freshers with very less to no experience, undergraduate students applying for internships, college, etc, back/front office job applications, part-time job applications.

COMBINATION RESUME FORMATS

The combination resumes are hybrid and custom resume formats. They use features of both chronological and functional resume to build custom profiles.

Features of a combination resumes Format

  • The combination resume is a custom format following chronology but not directly and including other information comprehensively.
  • This type of resume is used to highlight experience, learning, and achievements with an analytical/storytelling/detailed approach.
  • It is versatile with the customization that can be made but follows a list-based content approach. It is a very influential format if prepared correctly.
  • It is difficult to make and read as well. It is best used customized for every job application.

Recommended for:

Experienced professionals with a variety of experience, freelancers with a variety of results to show, professionals changing industry/profiles, freshers with extensive extra-curricular activities and results, and anyone who can combine different information (experience, learning, skills, results) together.

Resume formatting and layout tips

A professionally formatted resume always catches the right eyes. These simple rules can help you make your resume better:

  • Use left margin justification i.e. keep all text in the main body left-aligned.
  • Use single line spacing between the lines.
  • Use straight lines to break sections and also to give an outline to the resume, if you wish to.
  • Simple bullet points under each category head give a neat format.
  • For a hard copy, print on plain white or cream paper.
  • A resume should ideally be two pages in length and a cover letter strictly between half to one page.

Resume content tips

A good resume is defined by the format used and content layout. Here are some things to keep in mind while writing your resume:

  • Based on the job and application type along with your profile and experience, you can select any one of the three types of resumes.
  • Content should be crisp, clear, and targeted towards the application and job requirements.
  • Content should clearly present the skills, their relevance to the job, and their previous application if any.
  • Use relevant keywords and key phrases for digital and targeted information.
