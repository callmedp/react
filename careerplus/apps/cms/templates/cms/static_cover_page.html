Cover Letter Format

The cover letter is the document that accompanies your resume during the job application. It is a brief summary of your professional life and career. It is required for almost every job application, whether in print or digitally. The cover letter is often THE job application in itself. And the cover letter format is the tool to communicate the right information the right way and create a good impression.

A Cover letter allows you to target the job and the employer in a very specific way. Resume can be detailed but the cover letter is crisp and to the point, only hinting at the value you can bring to the table if hired.

Importantance Of Cover Letter?

The cover letter is an introductory note and conveys the objective of writing along with a few specific career highlights. The style of communication in the cover letter plays a vital role during the screening process. An impressive note pushes the reader to read more on your resume. Ensuring an appropriate format for your cover letter is the first step to a successful application.

Types Of Cover Letter Formats

Essentially, cover letters do not have different formats. But with technology and its effects, there are certain approaches one can use to grab attention and deliver necessary information. These approaches are dependent on the method of submission of a cover letter and also, on the requirements of the profile and the job. Here are the three main content types of cover letters:

Paragraphic Cover Letter Format

The most common and conventional format for cover letters, paragraphic refers to the delivery style of cover letter content in a simple descriptive format. The content can be divided into several paragraphs and delivered as per the information and can be molded for impact.

Features of Paragraphic Cover Letter Format:
  • The paragraphic format is simple, descriptive, and open to styling, composition, structure, etc.
  • The content can be planned and delivered as per the needs of the job application.
  • It is easier to skim through, familiar, and can be very impactful.
  • The format is followed almost everywhere as a standard.
Recommended for:

Experienced professionals and professionals with a diverse portfolio.

List-Based Cover Letter Format

It is one of the simplest formats for a cover letter and can be impactful in several specific cases. The delivery style is more direct and factual and relies on the value and influence of the information itself. The content is delivered in a bullet list or a numeric list with short, crisp, and to the point expressions.

Features of List-Based Cover Letter Format:
  • The list-based cover letter is a direct approach format with factual and direct information.
  • The content is readily available and needs very less customization for different job applications.
  • It can easily highlight achievements, awards, results, skills, etc and can create an impactful impression on readers.
  • It is not standard practice but is accepted almost everywhere due to its ease of reading.
Recommended for:

Freshers with a variety of co-curricular work and/or skills, Professionals in Sales, Business Development, Accounting and other result driven fields, Freelancers and Highly experienced professionals with a variety of work experience.

Combination Cover Letter Format

While the above two have their own targeted approaches, a combination cover letter incorporates both into a single structure for a better delivery. It is basically a custom format and the content, layout and other aspects can vary based on the individual needs. Apart from the above, it is often known to incorporate other elements like (info)graphics, tables, etc. which can have strategic advantages too.

How To Write A Cover Letter?

The objective should be very clear in the cover letter. A professionally sound cover letter has the following features:

  • Be very concise; put only relevant information. A single page cover letter is recommended.
  • Customize it as per the requirement of the employer and the job profile. A cover letter format is generally divided into three parts: Salutation and opening, introduction and brief, conclusion and closing.
    • Salutation and opening address the person and the purpose of communication, which is ‘job application’ here.
    • Introduction and brief is a short summary that brings forward relevant information regarding professional life. It explains your eligibility and competence towards the job prospect.
    • Conclusion and closing is the final note that projects the resolve and courtesy and provides contact information for further communication.

    How To Write A Cover Letter?

  • Make sure you do not repeat content from your resume. Put forward unique points with references to content from the resume.
  • Conclude with an optimistic outlook.
  • Give a call to action by clearly providing your email address and contact information with a positive closing note.

A well-formatted cover letter with sensible content projects a lot of positive vibes towards the job application. Choosing the right cover letter format saves a lot of time and effort and also lays a basic guideline to customize your job application to its specific needs. Creating a good impression in the first contact makes the further steps easier. So give your cover letter a little time and effort and start preparing for that interview!
